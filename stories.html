Award-winning Digital Arts

Digital storytelling platforms, developed with the support of Arts Council England, BBC, and Nottingham Trent University have helped hundreds of students and creatives find their voice and contribute to Nottinghamshire's rich cultural landscape.

Many of these projects have been a creative partnership with writer and educator James Walker. From our first meeting at the Nottingham Writers Studio in 2010, we have spent years developing the art of digital storytelling and celebrating the literary greats connected with our region.

After winning The Guardian Award for teaching excellence in 2015, we helped secure UNESCO City of Literature status for Nottingham. Projects like the zombie-genre digital comic 'Dawn of the Unread' - aimed at reluctant readers, or 'Sillitoe Trail' - a reimagining of the 1959 classic 'Saturday Night and Sunday Morning' have blended the written word with video and live events.

These innovative platforms, focussed on Nottinghamshire's iconic rebel writers have attracted interest from radio and television, The British Library, and leading universities.
