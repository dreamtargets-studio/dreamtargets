Adrian Reynolds

Contact

paul@thinkamigo.com

Adrian is a scriptwriter and catalyst who thrives on the creative possibilities presented by new collaboration. He is currently the editorial advisor on my new digital storytelling project Houdin Window.

We met in 2014 at the start of Dawn of the Unread. His role was to help organise the various contributors, Adrian also wrote the story 'Little Boxes' which was illustrated by Francis Lowe and set a high bar.

During Covid-19, Adrian produced a number of multmedia essays for Thinkamigo which tapped into pandemic paranoia. These dense, multilayered mysteries represented a taste of things to come.

I began to formulate a wartime story set in Sherwood Forest. But this was superceded by something that had been gathering dust in a garden shed for over ten years.

Puppetmaster was a dark comedic screenplay originally written with my old art school friend, TV puppeteer Marcus Clarke (Hotdog, Doogie Dog, Tickle and Patch, Buckaboo). The script and storyboard was rescued when I decided to use some elements as the basis for a serious cold war thriller - Houdini Window - my first attempt at long form fiction.

I've been developing characters and storylines using methods normally reserved for software development and service design. Only this time around I'm the writer instead of 'the digital guy'. Adrian has helped steer a clear path through the writing process, which isn't always easy. And we have big plans for Houdini Window in the months ahead.

Paul Fillingham
