Adrian Reynolds

Contact

paul@thinkamigo.com

Scriptwriter and catalyst. Adrian thrives on the creative possibilities presented by collaboration. He's currently the editorial advisor on my digital storytelling project Houdini Window

We met in 2014 at the start of Dawn of the Unread when his role was helping to organise the various contributors. Adrian also contributed the story 'Little Boxes' which was illustrated by Francis Lowe and set a high bar.

During Covid-19, Adrian wrote essays for Thinkamigo which tapped into pandemic paranoia: Dense, multilayered mysteries which represented a taste of things to come. Between pandemic Gov work, I was developing a wartime story set in Sherwood Forest. However, this was superceded by something that had been gathering dust in a garden shed for a decade.

Puppetmaster was a dark comedic screenplay originally written with old art school friend, TV puppeteer Marcus Clarke. Following discussions with Adrian these fragments became the basis for Houdini Window'Houdini

a serious cold war thriller and my first attempt at long form fiction, employing methods normally associated for software development and service design.

Only this time around I'm the writer instead of 'the digital guy'. Adrian continues to steer a path through the writing process, which isn't always easy. And we have big plans for Houdini Window in the months ahead.

Paul Fillingham
