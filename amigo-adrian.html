Adrian Reynolds

Scriptwriter and script editor

adrian.h.reynolds@gmail.com

Adrian thrives on the creative possibilities presented by collaboration. He's currently script editing my digital storytelling project Houdini Window.

We first partnered on Dawn of the Unread (2014), where his role was co-ordinating the comic writers and artists. Adrian also wrote the story and embedded content for 'Little Boxes' which was illustrated by Francis Lowe and set a high bar.

During Covid-19, Adrian wrote essays for Thinkamigo which tapped into pandemic paranoia. These dense, multilayered mysteries represented a taste of things to come. Between pandemic Government work, I was developing a wartime story set in Sherwood Forest. However, this was superceded by something that had been gathering dust in a garden shed for a decade.

Puppetmaster was a comedic screenplay originally written with old art school friend, TV puppeteer Marcus Clarke. Following discussions with Adrian these fragments became the basis for Houdini Window a serious cold war thriller - my first attempt at long form fiction.

We've been employing production methods normally associated with software development and service design, including the use of generative AI for storyboards and AI Language Models for mapping a timeline that spans seventy years. This time of course, I'm the writer instead of 'the digital guy'.

We have big plans for Houdini Window in the months ahead. Follow the Substack .
