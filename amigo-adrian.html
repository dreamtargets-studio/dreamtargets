Adrian Reynolds

Contact

paul@thinkamigo.com

Adrian is a scriptwriter and catalyst who thrives on the creative possibilities presented by new collaborations. He's a people person. Well read, an avid listener and viewer. He connects people in order to arrive at interesting creative outcomes.

I first met Adrian in 2014 at the start of the Dawn of the Unread project. James had recruited Adrian to help with rounding-up other writers, artists and filmmakers. He was also commissioned to work on one of the comics directly. - Adrian's vast knowledge of the graphic novel genre - the movers and shakers, and what constitutes a 'good story', illustrated by Francis Lowe set a high bar 'Little Boxes' and remains one of my favourite stories of the series.

We've shared many ideas over the years. During Covid-19 we produced a number of multmedia essays that tapped into the paranoia of the time. These dense, multilayered mysteries required some decoding from the reader and represented a taste of things to come.

In 2022, I began to formulate a wartime story set in Sherwood Forest. This process involved a lot of phonecalls, reading, emails and crafted timelines. But this wasn't the story that would eventually take hold.

A dark comedic screenplay devised with my old art school friend, TV puppeteer Marcus Clarke (Hotdog, Doogie Dog, Tickle and Patch, Buckaboo) had been gathering dust for over ten years, in my garden shed. The story was almost destroyed until I decided to rework it as a serious cold war thriller.

After many months, we arrived at the title 'Houdini Window' developing characters and storylines, and using the design framework and tools normally associated with software product development to create journeys, timelines and personas. This time around I'm the writer instead of 'the digital guy'. Adrian's role as story editor is to put me back on the rails whenever I deviate. It is a good partnership and Adrian is an excellent mentor and advisor, as many lucky creatives have discovered.

Paul Fillingham
