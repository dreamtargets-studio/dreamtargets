Adrian Reynolds

Contact

paul@thinkamigo.com

Adrian is a scriptwriter and catalyst who thrives on the creative possibilities presented by new collaboration. He is currently the editorial advisor on my new digital storytelling project Houdin Window.

We first met in 2014 at the start of Dawn of the Unread. James Walker recruited Adrian to help organise the contributors. Adrian wrote the story 'Little Boxes' which was illustrated by Francis Lowe and set a high bar for the Project.

During Covid-19, Adrian produced a number of multmedia essays for Thinkamigo that tapped into the paranoia of the time. These dense, multilayered mysteries represented a taste of things to come.

At this time, I began to formulate a wartime story set in Sherwood Forest. But this wasn't the story that would eventually emerge. Because that story was gathering dust in my garden shed!

Puppetmaster was a dark comedic screenplay devised with my old art school friend, TV puppeteer Marcus Clarke (Hotdog, Doogie Dog, Tickle and Patch, Buckaboo). The story was almost destroyed until I decided to use it as the basis for a serious cold war thriller - Houdini Window - my first attempt at long form fiction.

We've been developing characters and storylines, using the design framework and tools normally associated including several generative AI and Langage Model platforms. This time around I'm the writer instead of 'the digital guy'. Adrian puts me back on the rails whenever I deviate. He's an excellent advisor, as many lucky creatives have discovered.

Paul Fillingham
